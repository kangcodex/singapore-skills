# Sourcing Tracks — Supermarket vs Wet Market

Two parallel sourcing paths for the Singapore home chef. Each has a *place*, a *speech register*, an *ingredient format*, a *price band*, and a *use case*. Pick the track that matches your *time + budget + ambition*, not the one that matches the hawker you saw on TikTok.

## The two tracks

| Track          | Where you shop                          | What you say                              | What you get                        | Price band |
| -------------- | --------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ---------- |
| Supermarket    | NTUC FairPrice / Sheng Siong / Cold Storage / Giant / Don Don Donki | "Where is the [English name]?" | Pre-portioned, labelled, ready-to-cook | S$ — S$ +  |
| Wet market     | Tekka / Tiong Bahru / Geylang Serai / Chinatown Complex / Maxwell | "[Malay name] [quantity]"  | Whole, fresh, vendor-portioned      | S$  — S$   |

The two columns are deliberately inversed. **Supermarket = more money, less talk. Wet market = less money, more talk.** The *quality* difference is real (wet market is fresher, especially for herbs and seafood), but the *convenience* difference is also real (supermarket is 24/7, no haggling, no parking).

## Supermarket track (NTUC, Sheng Siong, Cold Storage, Don Don Donki)

### When to use this track

- You are a **beginner** and want labelled ingredients with barcodes
- It is **after 6 pm** and the wet market is closed
- You are cooking a **specific packaged dish** (e.g. "I want to make Prima Taste Laksa" — the kit tells you exactly what to buy)
- You need **pantry staples** (soy sauce, fish sauce, dark soy, Shaoxing wine) — these are the same brand for 99% of supermarkets in SG

### Where to shop

| Supermarket         | Best for                                                  | S$ tier     | Closest MRT      |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | ----------- | ---------------- |
| **NTUC FairPrice**  | Pantry staples, branded rempah, halal section             | S$ — S$ +   | Every HDB estate |
| **Sheng Siong**     | Pantry staples, slightly cheaper than NTUC, Chinese labels | S$ — S$     | Bedok, Tampines, Toa Payoh |
| **Cold Storage**    | Imported ingredients, organic, gluten-free, western brands | S$ +  — S$ ++ | Orchard, Novena, Bukit Timah |
| **Giant**           | Bulk buy, hypermarket                                      | S$          | Sengkang, Hougang |
| **Don Don Donki**   | Japanese ingredients, instant noodles, premium sauces    | S$ +  — S$ ++ | Orchard, City Square, Jem |

### What to say

At the supermarket, you are mostly *silent* — you walk, you read, you pick. If you need help, ask an NTUC staff "Where is the [English name]?" — most staff speak English and Mandarin. Sample sentences:

- "Where can I find dark soy sauce?"
- "Do you have halal chicken breast?"
- "Is the kangkung pre-washed?"

### What you get

- **Pre-portioned** — 200g garlic, 500g kangkung, 1.5kg whole chicken. No need to ask the vendor to portion.
- **Labelled** — every package has a barcode, a price, a brand, an expiry date. You can scan the price. You can compare brands.
- **24/7** — most NTUCs are 24/7. Sheng Siong opens 7 am–11 pm. Cold Storage is mall-hours (10 am–10 pm).
- **Halal-certified section** — most NTUCs have a *Foodfare* section with halal-certified meat and rempah.

### Price band

- **S$** (cheap): NTUC house-brand pantry, Sheng Siong basic rempah
- **S$ +** (mid): branded rempah (Prima Taste, Tean's, Ponniah)
- **S$ ++** (premium): imported (Don Don Donki, Cold Storage organic, gluten-free)

## Wet market track (Tekka, Tiong Bahru, Geylang Serai, Chinatown, Maxwell)

### When to use this track

- You are **intermediate or advanced** and want fresher ingredients
- You are cooking a **specific dish** where freshness matters (sambal, curry, assam)
- You want to **chat with the vendor** for tips (some of the best cooking advice in SG comes from a *makcik* at Tekka)
- You are buying **live seafood** (prawns, fish, crabs)
- It is **before noon** (most wet markets close by 1–2 pm)

### Where to shop

| Wet market              | Best for                                               | Address                                            | Open hours     |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------- | -------------- |
| **Tekka Market**        | Indian spices, fresh curry leaves, halal meat, fish    | 665 Buffalo Rd (Little India)                      | 6 am–3 pm      |
| **Tiong Bahru Market**  | Fresh pork, Chinese herbs, hawker-style produce        | 30 Seng Poh Rd                                     | 6 am–1 pm      |
| **Geylang Serai Market**| Malay rempah, fresh chilli, halal beef, *ikan bilis*   | 1 Geylang Serai                                     | 6 am–2 pm      |
| **Chinatown Complex**   | Dried goods, Chinese herbs, live seafood, *belacan*    | 335 Smith St                                       | 6 am–2 pm      |
| **Maxwell Market**      | Mixed — Malay, Chinese, Indian                         | 1 Kadayanallur St                                   | 6 am–2 pm      |
| **Balestier Market**    | Pork, tofu, Chinese herbs                              | 411 Balestier Rd                                    | 6 am–2 pm      |

### What to say

This is the *conversational* track. The vendor will recognise you by *what you ask for*, not by *how you phrase it*. Sample sentences in the three most common SG languages:

**English (you can use this everywhere):**
- "Can I have 200g of shallots, please?"
- "How much for one bunch of kangkung?"
- "Which chicken is the freshest today?"

**Malay (use at Geylang Serai, Tekka, Maxwell):**
- "Beri saya 200g bawang merah, 100g bawang putih, 5 batang serai."
- "Boleh kurang sikit? (Can you reduce a bit?)"
- "Pilih yang paling segar ya, makcik. (Pick the freshest one, please, auntie.)"

**Mandarin (use at Tiong Bahru, Chinatown, Balestier):**
- "请给我 200 克红葱头、100 克蒜头、五根香茅。" (200g shallots, 100g garlic, 5 stalks lemongrass)
- "这个多少钱？" (How much is this?)
- "帮我选新鲜一点的，谢谢。" (Help me pick the freshest, thanks.)

**Tamil (use at Tekka, Little India vendors):**
- "Sivappu vengayam 200g, poondu 100g, elumichai 5 kai kudunga." (200g shallots, 100g garlic, 5 lemons)

### What you get

- **Whole, fresh, vendor-portioned** — you ask for 200g shallots, the vendor pulls a bunch, weighs it on a balance scale, and hands it to you. No barcodes.
- **Haggling** — most wet-market prices are *negotiable* if you buy more than S$10. Don't expect 50% off, but a S$12 bill can become S$10.
- **Freebies** — the vendor will often throw in a *cilantro bunch* or a *lime* if you are a regular.
- **Freshness** — wet-market vegetables are picked that morning. The flavour is 2–3× supermarket.
- **Live seafood** — you can ask for a *live* fish and have it *cleaned* and *deboned* in front of you (some stalls charge S$2 for the cleaning, some don't).
- **Halal certification** — at Tekka and Geylang Serai, the meat stalls display a halal certificate from MUIS. At Tiong Bahru and Chinatown, the meat stalls are mostly *non-halal* (pork-friendly).

### Price band

- **S$** (cheapest): wet market is *always* cheaper than supermarket for fresh produce (typically 30–50% less).
- **S$ +** (mid): for live seafood, premium cuts (e.g. wagyu beef cheek), and herbs that the supermarket doesn't carry (e.g. fresh curry leaves, *daun kesum*).

## How the track maps to a single dish

Example: **Hainanese chicken rice.**

- **Beginner + supermarket (NTUC)**: 1 whole pre-cooked chicken (S$8) + 1 packet chicken rice seasoning paste (S$3) + 1 bag jasmine rice (S$4) + cucumber + garnish. Active time 25 min. Total cost ~S$17. Pantry impact: 1 paste jar (reusable for other dishes).
- **Intermediate + wet market (Tiong Bahru)**: 1 fresh whole kampong chicken (S$12) + 1 packet chicken rice paste (S$3) + 1 bag Thai Hom Mali rice (S$4) + 1 bunch pandan leaves (S$1) + cucumber + garnish. Active time 50 min. Total cost ~S$21. Pantry impact: 1 pandan bunch (single use).
- **Advanced + wet market (Tiong Bahru)**: 1 fresh whole kampong chicken (S$12) + 1 packet chicken rice paste (S$3) + 1 bag Thai Hom Mali rice (S$4) + 1 bunch pandan leaves (S$1) + 1 inch ginger (S$0.50) + 1 bunch scallion (S$1) + cucumber + garnish. Active time 90 min (incl. stock simmering). Total cost ~S$22.50. Pantry impact: small.

## Track contradictions

These are *real* mistakes that the script will warn you about:

- **Beginner + wet market + 30 min**: You are a beginner with no wet-market experience and only 30 min. The vendor will take 5 min to chat, you will overspend by 30%, and you will be late. **Use the supermarket track.**
- **Advanced + supermarket + fermentation ingredient**: You need fresh *belacan* for fermentation; the supermarket sells *paste belacan* in a jar. The flavour will be 30% of the fresh version. **Use the wet market track.**

If the script detects a contradiction, it returns a `warnings[]` array and the agent should respect the warning in the output.
