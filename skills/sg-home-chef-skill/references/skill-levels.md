# Skill Levels — Singapore Home Chef

Three-tier skill ladder calibrated for HDB-cookout-kitchen realities: single-burner induction, magnetic wok, no commercial-grade salamander, no sous-vide. Each tier maps a *technique ceiling*, a *time budget*, a *pantry philosophy*, and *what to outsource to shortcuts*.

## How to read this table

| Tier        | Time budget (active) | Equipment floor             | Technique ceiling                        | Rempah philosophy                       |
| ----------- | -------------------- | --------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Beginner    | 10–30 min            | Frying pan, 1 pot, spatula | One-pan / one-pot, simmer, no wok         | Outsource — buy ready-made paste        |
| Intermediate| 30–60 min            | Wok + spatula, steamer      | Tumis (shallow-fry paste), 2-pan timing  | Grind 50/50 — fresh + paste combo       |
| Advanced    | 60–240 min           | Carbon-steel wok, mortar, steamer, thermometer | Wok hei, fermentation, multi-stage timing | Grind fresh, manual rempah              |

## Beginner (≤30 min active)

**Profile.** New to the kitchen. Owns a frying pan, a pot, and a single ring. Has heard of *rempah* but has never pounded one. Probably owns a jar of sambal and a bottle of dark soy.

**What you can make well here.**
- Sambal Kang Kong (with Prima Taste / ready paste)
- One-pan Hokkien fried rice (char kway teow variant)
- Steamed egg with soy + sesame oil
- Quick Hainanese chicken rice (using chicken rice paste + pre-cooked rice)
- Any dish where the *wok hei* is optional and the protein is a single cut

**What you should NOT try yet.**
- Anything that depends on `wok hei` — you do not have the carbon-steel wok and you will not get the breath of the wok.
- Anything that requires *simultaneous* timing (e.g. char kway teow with prawns + egg + cockles).
- Anything with fermentation (belacan blocks, cincalok, shrimp paste paste-on-paste).

**Equipment floor.**
- Non-stick frying pan (24cm) or carbon-steel wok (12-inch, well-seasoned) — *either* is fine at this tier
- One pot (3 L) for noodles/eggs
- A spatula + a pair of chopsticks
- Measuring spoons (set of 4)
- A kitchen scale (1 g resolution, S$15 from Daiso)

**Rempah philosophy: outsource.** Use packaged rempah (Prima Taste, Tean's, Ponniah, or the supermarket house brand). The point of this tier is to learn *plating, seasoning, timing* — not paste consistency.

**Common mistakes (avoid these).**
- Adding raw garlic to a simmering dish at minute 0. It will burn by minute 4. Bloom it at minute 2.
- Cranking the induction to MAX for a "fast" fry. The pan will scorch. Use 7 of 9 for stir-fry.
- Putting a wet vegetable (kangkung straight from the wash) into hot oil. The water will spit. **Spin-dry in a salad spinner first.**
- Adding raw sambal paste to a cold wok. The paste will stick and burn before the oil is hot. Heat the wok, *then* the oil, *then* the paste.

## Intermediate (30–60 min active)

**Profile.** Comfortable with a wok, has a feel for `wok hei`, can hold two pans at once. Knows the difference between *dark soy* (甜) and *light soy* (鹹). Has done a paste or two in a blender, maybe a mortar.

**What you can make well here.**
- Hainanese chicken rice (whole chicken, from-scratch aromatics)
- Laksa lemak (paste either fresh-ground or 50/50)
- Char kway teow (single-burner, no cockles, with prawns + egg + lap cheong)
- Sambal stingray (with sambal tumis from scratch)
- Curry chicken (using powder + fresh aromatics)

**What you should NOT try yet.**
- Anything that requires *wok hei at full power* (1.5 m gas, 1.2 m kitchen, fan off) — you do not have the kitchen; you will not get the breath.
- Anything that requires *24-hour fermentation* (e.g. real assam fish head, fermented bean curd, shrimp paste secondary ferment).
- Anything that requires *manual rempah pounding* for > 100 g of paste (your wrist will give out; switch to a heavy-duty blender / food processor).

**Equipment floor.**
- Carbon-steel wok (14-inch, well-seasoned) — the magnetic induction-friendly one (e.g. Scanpan, Stahl, or S$30–60 from Phoon Huat / CK Cheong)
- Mortar (granite) + pestle for small-batch paste
- A blender (≥ 1 L capacity) for large-batch paste
- A steamer (bamboo 2-tier, S$12 at Daiso)
- A kitchen thermometer (S$15, probe-style, for oil and stock)
- A pair of long chopsticks / a wok spatula + ladle

**Rempah philosophy: blend.** Use 50/50 fresh + store-bought paste. Grind the *fresh* portion (shallots + garlic + ginger + lemongrass) in a blender with a splash of water; fold in the *store-bought* (e.g. Tean's sambal) at the end for the body and the chillies.

**Common mistakes.**
- Burning the paste. The *single* most common intermediate-tier failure. **Bloom at 5 of 9 heat, not 7 of 9.** The paste should *sizzle*, not *smoke*.
- Overcooking the protein. Chicken thigh at 3 min per side in a screaming-hot pan is *overcooked*. 90 seconds per side, then rest.
- Not salting the water. Noodles and rice both need salted water. The water should taste like sea water.
- Putting a wet wok back on the stove. It will sputter and the seasoning will crack. Wipe it dry first.

## Advanced (60–240 min active, multi-stage)

**Profile.** Has a carbon-steel wok seasoned to a mirror. Has done a multi-day fermentation or two. Understands that the difference between a hawker and a home cook is *technique discipline*, not equipment. Has the patience to do a 4-hour rendang.

**What you can make well here.**
- Roti prata (dough rested 4+ hours; flipping without a fork lift)
- Beef rendang (3 hours of slow reduction, 4 aromatics ground separately)
- Hokkien mee (prawn stock from shells; 2-wok timing)
- Assam fish head (assam gelugur + fresh tamarind; whole fish; spicy-sour balance)
- Char kway teow with cockles (live cockles, 3-second cook, hot wok)
- Laksa lemak (full paste from scratch; rempah pounded 30 min)

**What you CAN do (and the beginner cannot).**
- Achieve real *wok hei* (the breath of the wok) — requires a carbon-steel wok, a gas stove (not induction), a well-ventilated kitchen, and a wrist that can take 200 tosses.
- Manual rempah pounding (granite mortar + pestle; 30 min for 200 g of paste; the *sound* of the pestle should be a steady thump-thump-thump, not a crash).
- Fermentation (belacan blocks aged 6 months; cincalok; vinegar pickles; tau cheong).
- Multi-stage timing (3 woks at once; prawn shells in one, noodles in another, sauce in the third).

**Equipment floor.**
- Carbon-steel wok (14–16 inch, hand-hammered if budget allows; S$60–150 from Phoon Huat / Toa Payoh)
- Granite mortar + pestle (S$40–80, 20 cm diameter)
- Gas stove (1-burner portable, or 2-burner built-in; S$200+)
- A second carbon-steel wok (for parallel cooking)
- A steamer (2-tier, 30 cm, stainless)
- A kitchen scale (1 g resolution, S$25)
- A digital thermometer (S$20, for oil and stock)
- A spider / wire skimmer (S$8)
- A cleaver (8-inch, S$30) + a paring knife (S$15)

**Rempah philosophy: grind fresh.** Pound or blend fresh shallots, garlic, ginger, galangal, lemongrass, candlenuts, fresh chillies, and belacan. The 30 minutes you spend on the paste is the difference between a hawker-grade dish and a home-cook dish.

**Common mistakes.**
- Over-pounding the paste. The paste should be *coarse*, not a purée. You want to see 1–2 mm of granularity, not 0.5 mm of mush.
- Not blooming the paste in enough oil. The oil is the *medium* that carries the flavour. You need a 1:3 paste-to-oil ratio by volume.
- Walking away from the wok. A 4-stage rendang that needs 30 min of stirring every 5 min will *burn* if you step out for a phone call.
- Serving before the dish has rested. Curry tastes better after 10 min of rest. Laksa broth tastes better after 30 min of rest. The hawker knows this.

## How the tier maps to a single recipe

Example: **Sambal Kang Kong.**

- **Beginner + supermarket (NTUC)**: 1 packet Prima Taste sambal kang kong paste (S$5.50) + 1 bag kangkung (S$3) + 1 tbsp oil. Active time 10 min. Total cost ~S$8.50.
- **Intermediate + wet market (Tekka)**: Fresh kangkung (S$3) + homemade paste (shallot + garlic + dried shrimp + sambal) + 2 tbsp oil. Active time 25 min. Total cost ~S$7.00.
- **Advanced + wet market (Tekka)**: Fresh kangkung (S$3) + from-scratch rempah (shallot, garlic, belacan, fresh red chilli, calamansi juice) + Shaoxing wine deglaze + wok hei finish. Active time 35 min. Total cost ~S$8.50.

Same dish, three tiers, three *totally different techniques* — and three *totally different* outcomes. The home chef should pick the tier that matches their *time + equipment + patience* — not the tier that matches the *aspirational Instagram*.
